Introduction

In today's interconnected world, the concept of digital sustainability is gaining prominence. As digital technologies become increasingly integrated into our daily lives, it's vital to understand and embrace practices that minimise their environmental impact.

Digital sustainability encompasses strategies to reduce energy consumption, minimise electronic waste, and promote eco-friendly digital habits, ultimately aiming to create a more sustainable and environmentally conscious digital ecosystem. 

What is Digital Sustainability?

Digital sustainability refers to the responsible and environmentally conscious use of digital technologies and resources. It encompasses efforts to reduce the environmental impact of digital activities, such as internet usage, electronic device manufacturing, and data storage.

By adopting sustainable practices like energy efficiency, e-waste reduction, and digital carbon footprint awareness, individuals and organizations can contribute to a greener and more sustainable digital future.

Understanding Digital Carbon Footprints and Their Impact


Understanding Digital Carbon Footprints and their Impact is crucial in today's digital age. These footprints represent the environmental impact of digital activities, including internet usage and device manufacturing.

By comprehending their significance, individuals can adopt eco-friendly digital habits, reducing carbon emissions and promoting sustainability for a greener future.

Digital carbon footprints measure the environmental impact of online actions, highlighting the need for eco-conscious digital practices.

Minimising digital carbon footprints is essential for sustainability, fostering a greener future and reducing energy consumption in the digital realm.

Impact of Digital Consumption


The study titled "Climate crisis: The unsustainable use of online video – A practical case study for digital sobriety" (2019) was spearheaded by Maxime Efoui-Hess, an engineer with expertise in climate science and computer modelling. Efoui-Hess graduated from ISAE-SUPAÉRO, Université Paul Sabatier, and the French Meteorology University. He co-authored the report "Lean ICT – Towards a digital sobriety" (The Shift Project 2018).

Drawing from the online video case study, The Shift Project suggests a set of initial questions to be openly addressed, aimed at intelligently minimising the environmental impact of digital usage and promoting digital sobriety.
